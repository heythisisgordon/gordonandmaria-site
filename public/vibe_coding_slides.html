Why "Vibe" Coding?

Humans are excellent at abstracting systems. Abstraction is always a win/lose proposition—you gain understanding of the whole but lose sight of details.

For most people, the details don't matter.

"For architects and engineers who are really good at what they do and enjoy their work, there is always a strong echo of the enjoyment they got as they designed and built things in their childhood."

— Stuart Brown, Play

The Vibe Coding Mindset

🏗️ You Are the Architect

The LLM handles everything else. This is the fundamental shift that makes vibe coding work.

Your Role

System Design: Define what you want to build and how it should work
Context Management: Provide the right information at the right time
Quality Control: Review, test, and iterate on the LLM's output
Strategic Decisions: Choose tools, frameworks, and approaches

LLM's Role

Implementation: Write all the actual code
File Management: Create, organize, and maintain your codebase
Detail Work: Handle syntax, edge cases, and boilerplate
Research: Apply best practices and current patterns

Key Insight: Humans can't code an application from start to finish without concept, outline, revision, and testing. We shouldn't expect LLMs to either. Apply the same tools, systems, and processes to LLM-centric development.

Core Principles & Tools

Essential Principles

Never type code: You are not a coder, the LLM is. Always communicate intent, never implementation.
Never edit files directly: Tell the LLM what to change instead of making manual edits.
Copy/paste sparingly: Don't let the LLM boss you around. Use "Write instructions for another LLM" instead.
Context is king: Give enough context to work efficiently, but not so much you waste tokens.
Don't fiddle: Let the LLM finish its work before making changes or you might break things.
Stay in VS Code: Do as little outside your editor as possible to maintain flow.
LLM writes faster than you: Time spent typing while the LLM is dormant is wasted opportunity that kills the vibe.
